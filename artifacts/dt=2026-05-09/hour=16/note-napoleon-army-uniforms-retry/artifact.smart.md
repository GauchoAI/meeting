# Napoleon’s Army Uniforms (Retry)

Retrying creation via the shell-based artifact script. This note outlines line infantry characteristics, Guard and elite distinctions, branch and role differences, practicality and variation in the field, and the symbolism of uniform design in the Napoleonic era.

## Line Infantry
Common features included dark blue coats, colored facings, and evolving shako styles, with equipment standardized on paper but variable in practice.

## Guard and Elite Units
Imperial Guard and other elite formations often used distinctive headgear and trim to signal status and cohesion.

## Branch and Role Differences
Cavalry, artillery, engineers, and light infantry used differing cuts, colors, and gear suited to their roles.

## Practicality and Variation
Campaign wear, supply limits, and local replacement led to considerable variation from regulations.

## Symbolism
Uniforms supported identification, discipline, morale, and battlefield recognition.
