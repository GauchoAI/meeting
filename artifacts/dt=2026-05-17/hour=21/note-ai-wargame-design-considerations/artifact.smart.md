# AI Wargame Design Considerations

## Core consideration: prepared context is necessary

For an AI-arbitrated historical/geopolitical wargame, the system should **not depend on improvised internet search during play**.

Instead, each scenario should ship with a curated context pack:

- chronology of events
- orders of battle
- commanders and political actors
- maps and terrain notes
- logistics and supply constraints
- naval/transport capacity where relevant
- morale, command, and communication conditions
- known historical baseline
- plausible alternate branches
- source notes and confidence levels

The AI arbiter can then reason from this prepared material. If a fact is missing, it should declare uncertainty rather than inventing a precise answer.

## Why this matters

A strategic conversation or adjudication cannot work well if the arbiter lacks relevant facts. The model may be intelligent, but without prepared context it will be forced to rely on incomplete memory or quick lookup, which is unreliable.

## Example: British invasions of the Río de la Plata

A useful scenario pack would include:

- British force sizes for Montevideo and Buenos Aires
- ships and transports involved
- defensive arrangements of Montevideo and Buenos Aires
- role of Álzaga, Liniers, Whitelocke, Auchmuty, Stirling, etc.
- why Montevideo fell but Buenos Aires resisted successfully
- differences between fortress assault, urban defense, blockade, and political capitulation

Important discovered point: the British **did conquer Montevideo in 1807**, before the failed attack on Buenos Aires. That fact changes the plausibility space for alternate British strategies.
